# Moving Average Crossovers

Moving averages smooth recent price action into a trend line. They are lagging indicators, but they help reduce the noise from isolated price spikes and make support, resistance, trend direction, and trend failure easier to inspect.

For this crypto-options trader, use moving averages as context, not as a blind trading rule:

- A price crossing above a moving average can support a bullish interpretation, especially when volume/liquidity and option pricing make a call executable.
- A price crossing below a moving average can support a bearish interpretation, especially when the move is early enough for a put and not already exhausted.
- A short moving average crossing above a long moving average is a golden cross. It can support a call or a decision to avoid/close puts.
- A short moving average crossing below a long moving average is a death cross. It can support a put or a decision to avoid/close calls.
- The 9-period moving average is the short-term line. The 21-period moving average is the slower trend line.
- Do not trade the crossover alone. Confirm with recent candles, trend slope, bid/ask spread, option Greeks, implied volatility, open interest, and whether the expected move can overcome theta and spread cost.
- If the short and long averages are flat and repeatedly crossing, treat the market as choppy/range-bound and require better option pricing or stronger confirmation before opening exposure.
