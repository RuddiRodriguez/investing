# CAN SLIM Strategy Note

Source priority: primary strategy source for long-term stock CEO decisions.

Primary book reference: `/Users/ruddigarcia/Documents/William O'Neil - How to Make Money in Stocks_ A Winning System in Good Times and Bad, Fourth Edition (2009, McGraw-Hill Education) - libgen.li.pdf`

Curated user-provided source note:

CAN SLIM combines fundamental analysis, technical analysis, and market timing to identify exceptional companies before major advances.

C and A: Current and annual earnings matter. Prefer companies with very strong current and annual earnings growth. Triple-digit growth is especially important when available.

N: Look for something new: product, service, leadership, market, or other catalyst changing company character.

S: Supply and demand matters. Prefer high volume on up weeks and lighter volume on pullbacks, signaling institutional accumulation.

L: Prefer leaders, not laggards. Avoid the third or fourth best stock in a group. Relative-strength leadership is important.

I: Institutional sponsorship matters. Prefer accumulation by high-quality funds and other serious institutions.

M: Market direction is critical. Buy aggressively only when the general market is in a confirmed uptrend.

E: Earnings estimate revisions strengthen the setup when analysts are repeatedly raising current-year and next-year estimates.

Position sizing: protect the portfolio from one bad trade. Full positions are often 10% to 25%, but choppy markets or early rally phases should use pilot positions of 1%, 2.5%, or 5%. Scale exposure up only as trades work and scale down quickly when losses appear.

Buy points: do not buy blindly. Wait for bases and low-risk entries. Traditional entries include breakouts from cup-with-handle, flat base, and double-bottom patterns on above-average volume. Early entries can come from a break above a downtrend line in a handle or pullback. Pullbacks to 10-day, 21-day, or 50-day moving averages can be valid entries for leaders when institutional support is visible.

Risk rule: buy points should be close enough to a logical stop that the trade can be wrong within roughly 7% to 8%. If the current price is too far from the stop, avoid chasing.

Exposure: become aggressive after a follow-through day and constructive market behavior. Pull back when the market is choppy and breakouts fail quickly. Cash is a valid position in poor environments.

Execution principles:
- Enter with tight risk and let true alpha compound only after the trade works.
- Use anchored VWAP from important events such as IPOs, earnings gaps, or major lows to judge who controls the stock.
- Avoid overtrading highly volatile and unpredictable markets.
- Study price action and prefer stocks closing near the top of daily or weekly ranges.
- Cap position sizes to avoid portfolio concentration.
- Monthly charts can reveal accumulation/distribution and low-risk entry areas.
- Pullbacks to major support such as the 50-day moving average can provide safer entries than chasing extended prices.

Decision implications:
- Strong earnings growth plus new catalyst plus leadership plus institutional demand can support Buy.
- Rich valuation alone does not block a CAN SLIM setup, but price must offer a disciplined entry near a valid stop or breakout pivot.
- New IPOs can be valid only when they form a proper base or use a well-defined anchored VWAP / support entry.
- Failed breakouts, heavy distribution, weak market direction, or excessive distance from a stop favor Hold or wait-for-pullback.
- Dilution, negative free cash flow, or weak estimate support should reduce confidence unless the chart and institutional accumulation are exceptional.
