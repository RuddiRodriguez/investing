# Long-Term Stock And ETF Execution Guidance

Source priority: pinned guidance for stock and ETF CEO decisions.

Scope:
- Use this only for stock and ETF investing decisions.
- Do not use this for options or crypto decisions.
- Treat this as guidance for judgment, not as automatic hard rules.
- Reconcile it with business quality, valuation, news, market direction, chart structure, portfolio risk, broker limits, and user settings.

Business quality guidance:
- For individual stocks, check ROIC / return on invested capital when available.
- High and durable ROIC can support a stronger long-term business-quality read.
- Weak, falling, missing, or unclear ROIC should lower confidence unless other evidence explains why the business still deserves capital.
- Do not use ROIC alone. Reconcile it with growth, margins, balance sheet, cash flow, valuation, news, and chart setup.

Buy guidance:
- Do not use market orders for stock or ETF buys. Prefer limit orders.
- Do not chase a stock after a huge daily jump or extended price move.
- Do not buy a stock that is falling badly on bad business news.
- Do not buy more only because the price is cheaper. Averaging down needs a fresh business reason, a valid setup, and clear risk control.
- For the growth side, use at most one growth buy per week as guidance.
- For ETF accumulation, one ETF buy per month can be acceptable when it fits the plan and account risk.

Sell and review guidance for growth stocks:
- If a growth position falls about 7% to 8% from entry, review the thesis, chart, news, and risk. This is not an automatic sell, but it is a serious warning.
- If business news gets materially worse, sell or reduce unless there is a clear reason to keep holding.
- If the stock rises strongly and then momentum weakens, consider taking partial profit instead of assuming the move will continue.

CEO decision implications:
- A Buy should explain why the entry is disciplined and why a limit price is better than chasing.
- A Hold can be the right answer when the business is attractive but the current price is extended.
- A buy-lower plan is valid only when the lower price still has a good business thesis and risk/reward.
- Repeated growth buys should be rare. The CEO should mention weekly buy pacing when recommending additional exposure.
- ETF accumulation can follow a monthly rhythm, but it still must respect available cash and portfolio concentration.
